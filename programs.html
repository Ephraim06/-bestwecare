Explore Africa: The Discovery Expedition

While most students spend their breaks at home, Explore Africa invites Canadian high schoolers to step into a different world through a high-impact, stand-alone travel program designed for those seeking more out of their summer or winter holidays. Over the course of 2 to 4 weeks, students travel through the soul of Southern and East Africa—visiting Zimbabwe, South Africa, Zambia, Rwanda, and Namibia—where they transition from tourists to active participants in local life. Whether it is a missionary service trip, a milestone birthday celebration, or an unforgettable safari vacation, students will stand at the edge of the majestic Victoria Falls, engage in surf outreach and youth mentoring in South Africa, and perform meaningful missionary work in Rwanda while learning from the resilience and wisdom of local elders. The journey is packed with world-class safari experiences, from tracking the "Big Five" on sunrise drives and canoeing past hippos on the Zambezi to soaring in hot air balloons over the ancient Namib Desert and participating in hands-on wildlife conservation efforts. Under the world's clearest stars, students engage in authentic cultural exchanges, share traditional meals, and learn ancient bushcraft from local guides, ensuring every adventure is met with intention and a lifelong connection to the heart of Africa.

Who It's For

Students considering studying in Africa who want to experience African life, culture, and education firsthand, as well as those seeking meaningful travel experiences, missionary opportunities, or unforgettable adventure expeditions.

Timeline

2-4 weeks | Multiple intakes (June-August & December-January)

International Elementary School Program

JK – Grade 8 | Ages 4–13. A safe, structured, faith‑based environment with daily English language support and full integration into Ontario elementary schools. We partner with trusted schools across Ontario, where students receive a strong academic foundation plus caring guidance.

Ideal For

Families seeking safety and structure for younger students (JK–Gr8), parents who want Christian‑based education with strong support, and students needing English language development while attending school.

Living & Care

Approved homestay families or supervised boarding. Each student receives a local guardian who acts on behalf of parents, supports well‑being, and maintains regular communication. Safety first, always.

Academic & Language Support

Daily ELL support (conversation, grammar, reading, writing, homework help). Senior students attend English Language Academy twice per week after school.

University & College Pathway

Ontario is home to 20+ universities and 24 publicly funded colleges. We support students at every stage through two dedicated pathways: High School to University, and Graduate & Professional Advancement (2–4 years).

Pathway 1: High School to University

Ideal for high school students planning to attend university/college in Canada. We guide program selection, applications, study permits, pre‑arrival, and transition. Outcome: smooth, confident transition to Canadian post‑secondary.

Pathway 2: Graduate & Professional Advancement (2–4 years)

For graduates and working professionals seeking Canadian education to advance careers. We partner with colleges offering practical, career‑focused programs. Support includes program selection, admissions, visa guidance, accommodation, and settlement.
