Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our programs, admissions, and student life.

What programs does @BestWeCare offer?
We offer five core programs: High School (OSSD), Look See Canada (6-8 week immersion), School of Etiquette, University Support (undergraduate/master's), and For Science Lovers (summer lab program).
How long is the High School program?
Students typically complete 2–4 years depending on their entry grade. They graduate with the Ontario Secondary School Diploma (OSSD).
What is “Look See Canada”?
A 6-8 week summer immersion program where students experience Canadian life, attend camps, and explore nature – ideal for families considering longer studies.
What are the English language requirements?
Generally we accept IELTS 6.0+, TOEFL 80+, or equivalent. Some programs may have alternative requirements – contact us for details.
When are the application deadlines?
For High School: Semester 1 (Sep) deadline May 31; Semester 2 (Feb) deadline Oct 31. Science Lab: Apr 30. Other programs have rolling admissions.
What documents are required for application?
Valid passport, academic transcripts (translated), English test scores (if applicable), statement of purpose, reference letters, and medical insurance proof after acceptance.
Is there an application fee?
Yes, a non‑refundable fee of $169 USD is required before document review begins.
What kind of accommodation is provided?
We offer our own structured student accommodation – a true “home away from home” with full support, meal plans, and mentorship.
Do you help with airport pickup and settling in?
Absolutely. We coordinate airport pickup, help with SIN, health card, bank account, and accompany students to medical appointments if needed.
Are there activities outside academics?
Yes – we organise birthday/holiday celebrations, driving school support, first aid certification, and various social outings.
Do you provide visa assistance?
We offer comprehensive visa guidance and document support, though final approval is at IRCC’s discretion.
How can parents stay in touch?
We provide ongoing parent communication via email, WhatsApp, and scheduled video calls. You’ll never be out of the loop.
What if my child has a medical condition or dietary restriction?
We collect this information during application and work closely with families to accommodate needs – including medical appointment accompaniment.
Is there emergency support after hours?
Yes, current students have access to 24/7 emergency support. Contact details are provided upon enrollment.
